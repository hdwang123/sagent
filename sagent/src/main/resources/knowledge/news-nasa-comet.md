# NASA Study Reclassifies a Near-Earth Asteroid as a Comet

Published: July 16, 2026

Source: NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Source URL: https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/nasa-study-finds-near-earth-asteroid-is-actually-comet/

NASA-led researchers have concluded that the near-Earth object known as 1998 SH2
is actually a comet. Astronomers initially classified it as an asteroid because
older images showed no obvious tail or cloud of gas and dust. Its orbit later
began to differ from predictions based only on gravity, suggesting that another
force was changing its motion.

Researchers proposed that solar heating was turning ice inside the object into
gas. The escaping gas would act like a small thruster and produce the measured
changes in its trajectory. Observations from telescopes in Hawaii and Chile then
revealed a faint but clear tail, confirming the comet classification.

The result is relevant to planetary defense because accurate orbit predictions
depend on knowing whether an object can release gas. The study also suggests that
some so-called dark comets may be ordinary comets whose weak activity is difficult
to detect. Continuous tracking and precise position measurements can help identify
similar objects and improve estimates of their future paths.
