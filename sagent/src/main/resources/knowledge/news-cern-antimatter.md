# CERN Measures Matter-Antimatter Differences with Beauty Mesons

Published: July 17, 2026

Source: CERN, CMS Collaboration

Source URL: https://home.cern/cms-explores-subtle-matter-antimatter-difference-using-beauty-mesons/

The CMS Collaboration at CERN has reported a high-precision study of charge-parity
violation, a subtle difference between the behavior of matter and antimatter.
Understanding this effect may help physicists investigate why the observable
universe contains much more matter than antimatter.

The researchers studied neutral beauty mesons, particles that can transform into
their own antiparticles before they decay. CMS analyzed proton-proton collision
data collected from 2022 through 2025. The sample included about 1.4 million
neutral B mesons and 16,000 strange neutral B mesons.

An artificial-intelligence-based algorithm combined information from muons,
electrons, particle jets, and nearby collision products to estimate each meson's
initial state. The measured charge-parity violation agreed with the Standard
Model. The analysis also produced the most precise measurement so far for a
particular strange B meson decay involving a J/psi meson and a neutral kaon.
Future data from the High-Luminosity Large Hadron Collider should make these tests
even more sensitive to possible new physics.
