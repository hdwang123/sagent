# WHO Updates Guidance on Reducing Dementia Risk

Published: July 15, 2026

Source: World Health Organization

Source URL: https://www.who.int/news/item/15-07-2026-new-who-guidelines--up-to-45--of-dementia-risk-could-be-prevented-or-delayed

The World Health Organization has released updated guidelines for reducing the
risk of cognitive decline and dementia. WHO says that as much as 45 percent of
dementia risk may be linked to factors that can be modified or managed across a
person's lifetime.

The guidance recommends physical activity, stopping tobacco use, reducing alcohol
consumption, eating a healthy diet, remaining socially engaged, and using cognitive
training where appropriate. It also highlights the management of high blood
pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol, along with reducing exposure to air
pollution. Hearing aids may form part of a risk-reduction strategy for people who
need them.

WHO does not recommend vitamin B, vitamin E, omega-3, or general multivitamin
supplements solely to prevent cognitive decline when no deficiency has been
diagnosed. More than 57 million people currently live with dementia worldwide,
and nearly 10 million new diagnoses occur each year. The organization says health
systems should connect dementia prevention with services for noncommunicable
diseases, mental health, and brain health.
